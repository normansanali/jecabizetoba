    Miguel Cabrera Career

    Miguel Cabrera Career:- Cabrera made his major league debut on June 20, 2003, at the age of 20. The only Marlins player to do it earlier was Édgar Rentera, who did so at the age of 19. Cabrera hit his 100th career hit on April 20, 2004, a single with an RBI against Roberto Hernández in the seventh inning. Cabrera participated in the first World Baseball Classic on behalf of Venezuela prior to the 2006 season; the Venezuelan team placed seventh overall. On February 17, Cabrera won his arbitration hearing and went on to sign a one-year deal worth $7.4 million, becoming him the first player of the 2007 offseason to do so.

    In 2011, Cabrera, who had previously played in five All-Star Games, was selected as a reserve for the second AL All-Star squad. The Tigers revealed on March 28, 2014, that Cabrera had agreed to an eight-year deal extension worth $248 million. Due to a right hamstring strain, Cabrera was put on the 10-day disabled list on May 4, 2018. Cabrera hit his 3,000th career hit against Colorado Rockies pitcher Antonio Senzatela on April 23, 2022. It was an opposite-field single in the bottom of the first inning. Cabrera said at spring training in February 2023 that he would be calling it a career after that year.
