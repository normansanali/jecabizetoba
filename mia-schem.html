    All About Israeli French Woman Mia Schem 

    Mia Schem (age 21, born in 2002) is an Israeli-French woman abducted by Hamas. Reportedly, Mia was at a music festival near the Gaza Strip when Hamas terrorists abducted her. It’s believed that Mia is one of more than 200 people held against their will by this extremist group.

    Recently, Hamas released a video showing Mia, where she asked for help and reassured everyone that she was being taken care of and given proper food. The 59-second video also showed a doctor caring for her badly injured arm.

    Mia’s family is very worried about her safety. Her mother, Karen Schem, shared her concerns in an interview, talking about Mia’s kind and pure-hearted nature. Mrs. Karen urges the Israeli government to do everything possible to bring her daughter back home.

    Mia Skim Released From Hamas

    Mia Skeem, the 21-year-old French-Israeli woman who captured global attention after being abducted from the Supernova music festival in Israel and featured in a chilling Hamas hostage video, has been successfully released.

    The joyous reunion took place as Mia Skeem, who was training to become a tattoo artist, was freed along with seven other hostages in two separate releases on Thursday.

    The Supernova festival had taken an unexpected dark turn for Skeem, who was prominently showcased in a 78-second video on Hamas’s Telegram channel, revealing her injured arm being attended to by an unidentified medical professional. In the video plea, she urgently appealed to be reunited with her family at the earliest.

    The emotional scene unfolded as Skeem’s mother, Keren, who had tirelessly campaigned for her daughter’s release, was caught on camera by Israeli channel N12, overcome with tears of joy as she received the news and rushed to embrace her other children.

    Skeem’s father, David, expressed his overwhelming emotions, stating, “First, her mother is going to jump on her, and then I’ll come and hold her in my arms. I won’t say a word… I don’t want to ask questions. I don’t know what she’s been through.”

    In a heartfelt statement, the family expressed gratitude to everyone involved in securing Skeem’s release and urged for “calm and quiet” in the upcoming days as Mia undergoes medical treatment in the hospital before returning home.

    French President Emmanuel Macron also joined in the celebration, sharing his joy on social media, stating, “I share this great joy with her family and all French people.”

    Described as exceptionally creative on a campaign website advocating for her release, Mia Skeem was lauded by her mother, who remarked, “Everything she touches turns to gold.” Skeem, the second of four children, had attended the music festival with friends, including Elya Toledano, another French-Israeli who remains missing and presumed held hostage.
