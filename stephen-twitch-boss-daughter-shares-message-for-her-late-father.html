    Stephen tWitch Boss daughter shared a message for her late father on Tiktok. 

    Weslie Fowler, the 14-year-old daughter of Allison Holker and her late husband, Stephen “tWitch” Boss, has made a comeback on social media. 

    It is her first post since the death of her adoptive father in December 2022. Fowler took to TikTok to share a clip of herself in front of a mirror while listening to Mac Miller’s song “Surf,” with the caption, “i miss u.”

    Holker and many others commented to express their support and love for Fowler during this challenging time.

    Holker had confirmed Boss’s passing in a statement, saying, “Stephen lit up every room he stepped into. He valued family, friends, and community above all else, and leading with love and light was everything to him. He was the backbone of our family, the best husband and father, and an inspiration to his fans.” 

    Holker and Boss also share two other children, Maddox and Zaia.

    A private service was held for Boss’s family on January 4th. A more significant celebration of life was held in Los Angeles on February 11th, which 250 people attended. Weslie Fowler spoke at the event, moving attendees to tears with her heartfelt words.

    Holker expressed her gratitude for the support she and her family had received during this difficult time. 

    In a video posted on Instagram on February 18th, she said, “It has been very challenging and emotional, but you guys have brought so much hope and inspiration to us by sharing stories, and memories, and different ways that Stephen impacted your life. And it’s brought us so much hope and inspiration. He was someone that was just beautiful and he lived his life from love.”

    The death of Boss came as a shock to many. He was well-known for appearing on “The Ellen DeGeneres Show” and as a judge on “So You Think You Can Dance.” He was loved by fans for his infectious energy and positive spirit.

    Holker has also been sharing her journey of healing with her followers. She posted a photo of herself holding hands with her children on February 13th, writing, “We are strong, we are resilient, and we will always have each other.” 

    She has been open about her grief and the challenges she has faced since Boss’s passing.
