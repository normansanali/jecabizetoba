    Career

    Lil Romeo made his rap debut in 2001. He put out an album called “Lil Romeo.” The album sold over 200,000 copies in its first week. The following year, he released his next album. “Game Time” was the album’s title, and it peaked at number 33 on the Billboard 200.

    He released “Romeoland” in 2004. Although this album did not perform well, it did reach number 70 on the Billboard 200 music chart. With his father, he established Gutter Music Entertainment, a new record label. He also formed the rap group Rich Boyz with his brother Valentino Miller and three of his cousins, C-Los, Willie J, and Lil’ D.

    On November 22, 2005, the group released their debut album, “Young Ballers: The Hood Been Good to Us.” In 2006, he also released his digital album “Lottery.” Both albums were distributed by Gutter Music. Lil Romeo had changed his name to Romeo at the time, and the album “Lottery” was released under the new moniker. In the same year, he released “God’s Gift.”

    Following that, he released a number of mixtapes. Romeo dabbled in acting as well. He appeared in the 2001 film “Max Keeble’s Big Move” as a cameo. He appeared in the dance film “Honey” in 2003. He also appeared as a cameo in the film “Ned’s Declassified School Survival Guide.”

    He also appeared in the series “The Defenders” and the 2011 romantic film “Jumping the Broom.” He also launched his “College Boyys” clothing line. He wants young people to dress in clothes that make them feel successful, him. The clothing line was promoted by his “College Boyys” group and young artists such as Justin Bieber, Jaden Smith, and Big Time Rush.

    Romeo also wanted to play basketball. He also pursued a modeling career. He was a model for a new 2011 calendar. In 2011, he also modeled for TROIX magazine. He also appeared on the reality show “Dancing with the Stars.” He collaborated with Chelsie Hightower.

    Lil Romeo’s Achievements and Awards

    Romeo has three Billboard Awards, including Rap Artist of the Year in 2001. He was nominated for three Kids Choice Awards from 2004 to 2006, and he won the award in 2005. In 2004, he was nominated for Teen Choice Awards and Black Reel Awards for his performance in the film “Honey.”
