    Valorant, the popular first-person shooter game developed by Riot Games, features a complex ranking system that determines the skill level and progression of players. 

    Valorant Ranks

    Valorant ranks play a pivotal role in the competitive landscape of the game. The ranking system in Valorant is designed to measure and reflect the skill level of individual players, allowing them to compete against others of similar proficiency.

    The ranks are divided into several tiers, each representing a specific skill level, and are instrumental in determining a player's standing in the competitive community.

    All Valorant Ranks in Order

    Valorant features a total of 25 distinct ranks, each signifying a different level of skill and expertise. The ranks, in ascending order, are as follows:

    • Iron 1, Iron 2, Iron 3
    • Bronze 1, Bronze 2, Bronze 3
    • Silver 1, Silver 2, Silver 3
    • Gold 1, Gold 2, Gold 3
    • Platinum 1, Platinum 2, Platinum 3
    • Diamond 1, Diamond 2, Diamond 3
    • Ascendant 1, Ascendant 2, Ascendant 3
    • Immortal 1, Immortal 2, Immortal 3
    • Radiant

    It is important to note that the addition of the Ascendant tier was introduced later to balance the rank distribution curve and enhance the overall stability of the ranking system.

    Valorant Act Rank

    In addition to the overall rank, players also earn a separate Act rank as they progress through each act. The Act rank is determined by the highest-ranked win achieved during the act, known as the "proven skill".

    This Act rank influences the rewards received at the end of the act, providing an additional layer of recognition for players' accomplishments during specific periods of gameplay.

    Valorant Rank Distribution

    Understanding the rank distribution in Valorant is crucial for gauging the competitive landscape and gaining insight into the skill distribution of players. As of January 2024, the rank distribution in Valorant is as follows:

    • Iron: 9.2%
    • Bronze: 22.9%
    • Silver: 26.5%
    • Gold: 19.1%
    • Platinum: 12%
    • Diamond: 7.5%
    • Ascendant: 2.1%
    • Immortal: 0.4%
    • Radiant: 0.01% 

    These statistics provide a clear picture of the distribution of players across different ranks, with the majority of players falling within the Bronze to Gold range. Moreover, it highlights the elite status of the Immortal and Radiant ranks, which are reserved for the top echelon of players.

    The Top 1% in Valorant

    Based on the provided rank distribution data, it is evident that the top 1% of players in Valorant are those who have achieved the prestigious Radiant rank. With only 0.01% of players attaining this rank, it stands as the pinnacle of competitive achievement within the game.

    The Radiant rank is reserved for the most skilled and dedicated players, representing the highest level of proficiency and expertise in Valorant.

    Achieving the Radiant Rank

    Earning the Radiant rank in Valorant is a monumental accomplishment that requires exceptional skill, dedication, and strategic prowess. Players who reach this elite tier are recognized as among the best in the game, showcasing unparalleled proficiency and mastery of the game's mechanics.

    Progression and Restrictions

    Progressing through the ranks in Valorant involves navigating various restrictions and placement requirements. Players must adhere to specific guidelines, such as account level thresholds and placement match obligations, to access and advance within the competitive ranking system.

    Placement Matches and Progression

    To initiate ranked play, players must attain an account level of at least 20 and complete a set number of placement matches. The outcome of these matches, along with the player's MMR, influences their initial rank and subsequent progression within the system.

    Furthermore, the progression system ensures that players are placed conservatively after each reset, allowing them to work their way up the ranks based on their performance and skill level.

    Party Restrictions and Disparity

    Valorant imposes specific restrictions on party compositions to maintain a balanced and fair competitive environment. These restrictions vary based on the ranks of the players within a party, ensuring that disparities in skill levels do not compromise the integrity of ranked matches.

    Matchmaking Rating (MMR) and Competitive Play

    The matchmaking rating (MMR) in Valorant serves as a fundamental component of the competitive matchmaking system. MMR is a dynamic metric that adjusts based on a player's performance, influencing their placement in matches and overall rank progression. The intricate nature of MMR and its correlation with rank rating (RR) underscores the complexity of Valorant's competitive ecosystem.

    MMR and Rank Rating Gains/Losses

    The relationship between MMR and RR dictates the rate at which players ascend or descend within the ranking system. While MMR remains concealed from players, it significantly impacts their visual rank and the allocation of RR based on match outcomes.

    This interplay between MMR and RR ensures that players are matched against opponents of comparable skill levels, contributing to the integrity and competitiveness of ranked matches.

    Convergence and Rank Progression

    The concept of convergence plays a pivotal role in the progression of a player's rank towards their MMR. As players prove their proficiency and consistently perform at a higher level, their RR reflects this by facilitating their ascent towards the upper bounds of their rank estimate.

    Convergence signifies the alignment of a player's rank with their MMR, signifying a relatively even distribution of RR gains and losses.

    Overall, the Valorant ranking system is a multifaceted framework that encompasses a diverse range of ranks, each indicative of varying skill levels and expertise.

    The elite status of the Radiant rank solidifies its position as the top 1% in Valorant, representing the pinnacle of competitive achievement within the game.
