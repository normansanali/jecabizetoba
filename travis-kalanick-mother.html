    Uber boss Travis Kalanick’s mother dies in boating accident

    Bonnie Kalanick, the mother of Uber CEO Travis Kalanick, has died in a boating accident. Bonnie Kalanick, 71, perished when the boat she and her husband were aboard collided with a rock in Pine Flat Lake near Fresno, California. The Northridge couple has been long-time boat owners.

    In a letter to Uber employees, Liane Hornsey, the company’s chief human resources officer, described the occurrence as an “unimaginable tragedy.” She stated that “everyone in the Uber family knows how incredibly close Travis is to his parents.”

    Travis Kalanick’s parents

    Travis Kalanick was born on August 6, 1976, and grew up in Northridge, California. Travis’ parents are Bonnie Renée Horowitz Kalanick (née Bloom) and Donald Edward Kalanick.

    Bonnie’s family were Viennese Jews who immigrated to the United States in the early 20th century and worked in retail advertising for the Los Angeles Daily News.

    Kalanick grew up in a Slovak-Austrian Catholic family with ancestors who emigrated to the United States from the Austrian city of Graz.

    He was a civil engineer for the city of Los Angeles, born and raised in Los Angeles, California. Kalanick has two half-sisters: one is Ashley Arm’s mother, while his brother is a firefighter.

    During his middle and high school years, Kalanick was notorious for being competitive and determined to succeed. Kalanick sold Cutco knives door-to-door as a teenager. With the father of a classmate, he started “New Way Academy,” an exam preparation business, at the age of 18.
