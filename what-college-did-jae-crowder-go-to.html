    Jae Crowder is a professional basketball player in the NBA. He attended multiple colleges throughout his collegiate career, but his most notable time was spent at Marquette University. In this article, we will explore Crowder's college journey, his time at Marquette, and how his college experience shaped his career in the NBA.

    Jae Crowder's College Career

    Jae Crowder, full name Corey Jae Crowder, was born on July 6, 1990, in Villa Rica, Georgia. He began his basketball journey in high school, where he showcased his skills and caught the attention of college recruiters. After completing his high school career, Crowder had several college offers to choose from.

    Crowder's First College: South Georgia Tech

    Crowder's college journey started at South Georgia Technical College, a junior college located in Americus, Georgia. He played for the South Georgia Tech Jets during the 2008-2009 season. Crowder had a successful year, averaging 18.6 points, 9.7 rebounds, and 2.9 assists per game. His performance earned him the distinction of being named the Georgia Junior College Athletic Association (GJCAA) Player of the Year.

    Transferring to Howard College

    After one year at South Georgia Tech, Crowder transferred to Howard College in Big Spring, Texas. He played for the Howard College Hawks during the 2009-2010 season. Crowder continued to impress on the court, averaging 18.9 points, 9.0 rebounds, and 2.3 assists per game. His contributions helped lead the Hawks to a successful season, including a conference championship.

    Jae Crowder's Time at Marquette University

    Following his successful two-year stint at junior college, Crowder transferred to Marquette University in Milwaukee, Wisconsin. He joined the Marquette Golden Eagles for the 2010-2011 season, playing under head coach Buzz Williams. Crowder made an immediate impact, becoming a key player for the team.

    During his senior year at Marquette in the 2011-2012 season, Crowder had a breakout year. He averaged 17.5 points, 8.4 rebounds, and 2.1 steals per game. Crowder's exceptional performance earned him the Big East Player of the Year award, solidifying his place as one of the top college basketball players in the country.

    Success and Achievements at Marquette

    Crowder's contributions were instrumental in Marquette's success during his time there. The team reached the Sweet Sixteen of the NCAA Tournament in both the 2010-2011 and 2011-2012 seasons. Crowder's leadership and skills were crucial in guiding the team to these deep runs in the tournament.

    In addition to being named the Big East Player of the Year, Crowder also received All-Big East First Team honors in his senior year. He left a lasting impact at Marquette, not only through his on-court performance but also with his leadership and work ethic.

    Impact of College on Crowder's NBA Career

    Crowder's time in college played a significant role in shaping his career in the NBA. His experiences at South Georgia Tech, Howard College, and Marquette provided him with the necessary skills and exposure to catch the attention of NBA scouts.

    In the 2012 NBA Draft, Crowder was selected as the 34th overall pick by the Cleveland Cavaliers. However, he was immediately traded to the Dallas Mavericks, where he started his professional career. Crowder's college success and the skills he developed during his time at Marquette helped him transition smoothly to the NBA.

    Jae Crowder's college journey was a crucial stepping stone in his basketball career. Starting at South Georgia Tech and Howard College, Crowder showcased his talent and developed his skills. However, it was during his time at Marquette University that he truly made a name for himself.

    Crowder's outstanding performance at Marquette, including being named the Big East Player of the Year, opened doors for him in the NBA. His college experiences provided him with the necessary foundation to excel at the professional level. Today, Crowder continues to make an impact in the NBA, and his college journey remains an integral part of his basketball story.
