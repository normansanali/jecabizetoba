    Who is Boosie Badazz?

    Boosie Badazz is an American rapper and songwriter. He is popular for his single “Set It Off”.

    Likewise, he came into the limelight through his album “Touch Down 2 Cause Hell” and “SuperBad: The Return of Boosie Bad Azz.”

    Who are the parents of Boosie Badazz?

    Boosie Badazz was born on November 14, 1982, in Baton Rouge, Louisiana, United States of America.

    How Old is Lil Boosie?

    Likewise, his real name is Torrence Hatch Jr. As of 2023, he celebrated his 40th birthday.

    Boosie is the son of Torrence Hatch, Sr., and Connie Hatch. Likewise, his father was a drug addict while his mother was a school nurse. His father, Torrence Hatch Sr., died in 1997.

    Later, he also started to involve in drugs. He has 7 siblings. He grew up in an area full of drug dealings and gunplay.

    His nationality is American and his ethnicity is African-American. Furthermore, his zodiac sign is Scorpio.

    He was kicked out of the school because of his drug-related activities.

    Career, Professional Life

    Boosie was interested in music since his childhood.  He was already in the ‘Concentration Camp’ group in 1996, at the age of 14. 

    Boosie debuted on C-Loc’s fifth album, ‘It’s A Gamble’ in 2000.

    Likewise, he assisted Concentration Camp’s third studio album ‘Camp III: Thug Brothas’ in the same year and he began working in the prime position of the group.

    In the same year, he recorded his debut album, ‘Youngest of da Camp.’  He collaborated with songwriters and singers like Webbie, Pat Lorenzo, and DJ Mustard.

    Till now, he has released many popular songs such as United We Stand, Divided We Fall, Street Code, and Show Da World among others.

    Moreover, he has acted in movies like ‘Gangsta Musik, and ‘Last Dayz, and Ghetto Stories: The Movie’ as a drug dealer.

    Rumors and Controversies

    Boosie was arrested on October 22, 2008, when police found marijuana and a gun in his car, and was sentenced to two years in prison.

    On June 17, 2010, he was charged with the first-degree murder of Terry Boyd and faced charges of possession of narcotics.

    But in 2012, he was found not guilty of the first-degree murder of Terry Boyd. On November 29, 2011, he was again prisoned for eight years for drug charges.
